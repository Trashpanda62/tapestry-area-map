    📋 The full playbook

    The one rule: ~75–90% cute animals, entertainment & connection · ~10–25% “you can stay here.” People follow for the alpacas; they book because the experience and the reviews earned their trust. Everything below is how you run that.

    Each feed has one job

    TikTok · @tapestry.acres

    The engine. Your real audience lives here. Where new people discover the farm — give it the most time.

    Instagram · @tapestryacres

    The storefront. Where someone checks “is this place legit?” right before they book.

    Facebook · /tapestryacres

    The local board. Older, nearer, more ready to book. Events & Tennessee travel/RV groups.

    2 · How to run each feed

    TikTok — the growth engine

    • 4–7 posts a week — it rewards volume & consistency.
    • Keep it lo-fi. Wrap promos inside an animal moment.
    • Cute hook in the first second; use trending sounds.
    • Pin 2–3 videos up top (one “book a walk,” one “glamping soon”).
    • Reply with a video — free extra reach.
    • Say where you are: “2 hrs from Nashville,” “near Dale Hollow Lake.”

    Instagram — the storefront

    • 3–5 Reels a week + Stories every day.
    • No watermark — export from CapCut/originals, never a TikTok download.
    • Re-write the caption for IG; drop #fyp.
    • Build Story Highlights (you have 0): Stay · Book Now · Glamping (soon) · Animals · Reviews · Events.
    • Carousels for the stay experience — the most saves.
    • Always tag Monroe, TN.

    Facebook — local & booking

    • 3–4 posts a week — photo + a story-style caption + a booking link.
    • Use Facebook Events for anything happening on-site.
    • Share into local / TN travel & RV groups — huge free reach.
    • Reply to every comment & message quickly.
    • Fix the stale bio (“moving to TN” → arrived + glamping coming).

    Film once, post everywhere — but re-write the caption for each app (short + emoji on IG/TikTok, a bit more story + a clear link on FB) and strip the watermark for IG. One farm walk = 10+ clips = a full week across all three.

    7 · Turn views into bookings

    Fix the funnel

    • One tracked link in all three bios (Linktree / Stan) → the Alpaca Walk ($50), your best actually-live product. Add cabins, then tents, as each becomes bookable.
    • Stand up a tent waitlist (free Wix form) and link it — bank the demand now.
    • Build the IG Story Highlights row — Stay, Book Now, Glamping (soon), Animals, Reviews, Events.
    • Rewrite all three bios — what you are + where + a call to action, each pointing at the others.

    Keep the engine fed

    • Batch-film. One walk = a week across all three feeds.
    • Don't go quiet. Your one real problem is cadence — keep something posting every few days.
    • Ask every guest for a Google review (4.3★ → 4.5★ / 25+) — cheapest, best-converting content there is.
    • Hook in the first second; wrap every promo in an animal moment.
