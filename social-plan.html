Facebook & Instagram Posting Plan

The right mix of post types to grow reach, build trust, and fill bookings — alpacas, glamping & RV sites.

The rule: ~90% value, entertainment & connection  ·  ~10% direct selling. People follow for the alpacas; they book because the experience and the reviews made them trust you.

2 · How to post it (format by platform)

Instagram
Reach lives in Reels & Stories

  • 50%+ Reels — 15–30s alpaca moments, glamping reveals, "a day at the farm." Non-negotiable for new-audience growth.
  • ~25% carousels — property tours & educational; these get the most saves.
  • ~25% single photos.
  • Stories daily — polls, "which alpaca is this?", availability countdowns, booking-link stickers, guest reposts.

Facebook
Older, more local, more booking-intent

  • Video still wins, but lean more on photo + story-driven captions and link posts to the booking page.
  • Use Facebook Events for anything on-site.
  • Share into local / TN travel & RV groups — outsized free local reach.
  • Reply to every comment & DM fast — FB rewards engaged Pages and that's where questions convert.

Cross-post, but re-caption for each — shorter & emoji-forward on IG, a bit more narrative with a clear link on FB. Don't auto-share IG→FB; native posts get more reach on both.

4 · The one thing to lean into

You already have named, photographed animals. Build recurring series around them:

  • "Meet the Herd Mondays" — spotlight one alpaca a week.
  • Name-the-baby polls & "guess who" Stories.
  • A single alpaca's POV Reel series.

Recurring formats train both the algorithm and the audience — and they're the cheapest content to make, because the cast never changes.
