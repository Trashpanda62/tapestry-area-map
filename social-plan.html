Social Media Posting Plan

One plan for all three feeds — Facebook, Instagram & TikTok — to grow the audience, build trust, and fill bookings.

The rule: ~75–90% cute animals, entertainment & connection · ~10–25% "you can stay here." People follow for the alpacas; they book because the experience and the reviews made them trust you.

Each feed has one job

TikTok · @tapestry.acres

The engine. The real audience (~12–14K). Where new people find the farm. Priority for time.

Instagram · @tapestryacres

The storefront. Where a guest checks "is this place legit?" before they book a stay.

Facebook · /tapestryacres

The local board. Older, nearer, more booking-intent. Events & TN travel/RV groups.

2 · How to run each feed

TikTok
The growth engine

  • 4–7 posts / week — rewards volume + consistency.
  • Lo-fi beats polished. Wrap promos inside an animal moment.
  • Cute hook in the first second; trending sounds.
  • Pin 2–3 videos up top (one "book a walk," one "glamping soon").
  • Reply with video — free reach.
  • Geo-caption: "2 hrs from Nashville," "near Dale Hollow Lake."

Instagram
The booking storefront

  • 3–5 Reels / week + Stories daily.
  • Watermark-free — export from CapCut/originals, never a TikTok download.
  • Re-caption native; drop #fyp.
  • Build Story Highlights (0 today): Stay · Book Now · Glamping (soon) · Animals · Reviews · Events.
  • Carousels for the stay experience — most saves.
  • Always geo-tag Monroe, TN.

Facebook
Local & booking-intent

  • 3–4 posts / week — photo + story-driven captions + booking links.
  • Use Facebook Events for anything on-site.
  • Share into local / TN travel & RV groups — outsized free reach.
  • Reply to every comment & DM fast.
  • Fix the stale bio ("moving to TN" → arrived + glamping coming).

Film once, post everywhere — but re-caption per platform (short + emoji on IG/TikTok, a bit more narrative + a clear link on FB) and strip the watermark for IG. One farm walk = 10+ clips = a week across all three.

7 · Turn views into bookings (do these first)

The funnel fixes

  • One tracked link in all three bios (Linktree / Stan + UTM) → the Alpaca Walk ($50) — the best actually-live product. Graduate to cabins, then tents, as each becomes bookable.
  • Stand up a tent waitlist (free Wix form) and link it — bank the demand now.
  • Build the IG Story Highlights shelf — Stay, Book Now, Glamping (soon), Animals, Reviews, Events.
  • Rewrite all three bios — value prop + location + CTA, each pointing at the others.

Keep the engine fed

  • Batch-film. One walk = a week across all three feeds.
  • Don't go quiet. The current gap is cadence — keep something posting every few days.
  • Ask every guest for a Google review (4.3★ → 4.5★ / 25+) — cheapest, best-converting content.
  • Hook in the first second; wrap every promo in an animal moment.
